## 1. THE BUSINESS — Understanding the Economic Engine

### Business Model

Evans Electric Ltd was incorporated in **1951** — a 75-year-old company — in the business of electro-mechanical repairs and servicing. It is a **niche, specialised industrial services company** that repairs, refurbishes, and maintains large Motors, Generators, and Transformers used in heavy industries like power generation, steel, cement, and other process industries.

**The business model is elegantly simple:** Heavy industries rely on massive electrical machines (motors, generators, transformers). These machines are extremely expensive to replace — often costing crores — but they can be repaired, rewound, and refurbished for a fraction of the replacement cost. Evans Electric is the specialist who does this work. As these machines age, the scope for repair and maintenance *increases*, creating a naturally recurring and growing demand base.

**Revenue Split (FY24):**
- Income from repair contracts: ~97%
- Gain on sale of investments: ~2%
- Other Operating Revenue: ~1%
- Geographic: Domestic ~99%, Overseas ~1%

**Services Offered:**
- In-house repairs, rewinds and re-designs on machines up to 50 tons
- On-site repairs and rewinds on larger machines
- High voltage generator and motor coil manufacture (up to 22KV)
- Roebel bar manufacture (up to 33KV)
- Transformer coil manufacture (oil cooled & dry type)
- Spare parts and specialised replacement component manufacture
- Advanced diagnostics, high voltage testing, dynamic balancing
- **Reverse engineering** — repairing machines where original drawings are unavailable

**Manufacturing Capacity:** 30,000 sq. ft. facility at Palghar with up to 50-ton lifting capacity.

**Would Buffett understand it?** Absolutely. This is one of the simplest businesses imaginable — fix broken industrial machines. A child can understand it. The machines MUST be repaired (downtime is catastrophic for heavy industries), and there are very few specialists who can do this work on large, high-voltage equipment.

> *Buffett: "I try to invest in businesses that are so wonderful that an idiot can run them. Because sooner or later, one will."*

**What type of business is this?** This is a **switching-cost business** with elements of a **niche monopoly**. When a steel plant's 50MW turbogenerator breaks down, they cannot call just anyone — they need a specialist with decades of experience, the right equipment (50-ton cranes, high-voltage testing rigs), and deep institutional knowledge of these machines. The switching cost is not financial — it is **competence-based**. Very few companies can do this work, especially the reverse engineering of machines where original drawings no longer exist.

The company provides **import substitution** on costly foreign-made apparatus and does in-house development and manufacture of costly replacement components. This is a moat — domestic, cost-effective expertise replacing expensive foreign OEM services.

**Order Book (Recent):**
- Sep 2024: ₹10.75 Cr order from Mahagenco (Ghatghar Hydro Power Station)
- Jun 2024: ₹5.20 Cr order from Bokaro Power Supply Company (SAIL/DVC JV)

These are government/PSU clients — large, creditworthy, and sticky.

## 7. COMPETITIVE POSITION & MOAT ANALYSIS

### Moat Assessment

**Moat Type: SPECIALISED KNOWLEDGE & SWITCHING COST MOAT — Narrow but Deep**

**Moat Sources:**

1. **Specialised Expertise (75 years of accumulated knowledge):** Evans Electric has been repairing large electrical machines since 1951. This expertise — understanding the quirks of different machine types, failure modes, and repair techniques — is accumulated over decades and is nearly impossible to replicate quickly. The **reverse engineering** capability (repairing machines where original drawings don't exist) is particularly rare.

2. **Equipment & Infrastructure Moat:** A 30,000 sq ft facility with 50-ton lifting capacity, high-voltage testing rigs, and specialised machining tools. This is not something a new entrant can set up cheaply or quickly.

3. **Switching Costs:** When a power plant's critical generator breaks down, they CANNOT experiment with an untested repair shop. The downtime cost per day (lost generation revenue, penalties) dwarfs the repair cost. Clients stick with proven specialists like Evans Electric because the risk of switching is asymmetric — a failed repair could cost 100x more than the fee premium of using a trusted specialist.

4. **Import Substitution Moat:** Evans provides domestic, cost-effective alternatives to expensive foreign OEM repair services. As India's installed power capacity ages and OEM support contracts expire, demand for domestic specialists like Evans increases.

5. **Relationship Moat:** Government/PSU clients (Mahagenco, SAIL, DVC, BHEL-adjacent work) represent sticky, repeat business. These clients have approved vendor lists that are very difficult to get onto.

6. **Labour & Knowledge Barrier:** Skilled electro-mechanical technicians who can work on high-voltage machinery are scarce. Evans has built this talent pool over decades.

**Moat Weaknesses:**

1. **Small scale** — The company is tiny (₹25 Cr revenue). It could be overshadowed or acquired by larger engineering firms.
2. **Client concentration risk** — A few large orders dominate revenue in any given year.
3. **Dependent on key personnel** — The recent CFO resignation (Feb 2026) and Factory Manager change (Jan 2026) show management transition risks.

### Key Competitors

| Competitor | Threat Level | Nature |
|---|---|---|
| Original Equipment Manufacturers (ABB, Siemens, BHEL) | Moderate | Expensive, but have brand and scale |
| Unorganised local repair shops | Low | Cannot handle large, high-voltage equipment |
| Other specialised shops (few in India) | Moderate | Limited number; market big enough for several |

### Susceptibility to Change

> *Buffett: "We do not get enthused about change... we're looking for the absence of change to protect ways that are already making a lot of money."*

**This business is BEAUTIFULLY resistant to change.** Large motors, generators, and transformers have looked essentially the same for 50+ years. The underlying technology (electromagnetic principles, insulation systems, winding techniques) evolves slowly. There is no AI, no software disruption, no digital transformation that threatens this business. As long as heavy industries use large electrical machines — which they will for decades — Evans Electric's services will be needed.

If anything, change HELPS Evans Electric:
- As India's industrial base expands, the installed base of large electrical machines grows
- As machines age, repair demand increases
- As foreign OEMs exit aftermarket support, domestic specialists become MORE valuable
- As environmental regulations tighten, refurbishment (vs. replacement) becomes preferred

**This is a 20-year thesis.** The business will look essentially the same in 2046 as it does in 2026.
