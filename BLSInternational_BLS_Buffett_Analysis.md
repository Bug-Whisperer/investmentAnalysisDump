## 1. THE BUSINESS — Understanding the Economic Engine

BLS International Services Ltd is the world's largest listed visa application outsourcing company. Founded in 1983 (originally Welken Traders), the company has evolved into a trusted tech-enabled services partner for governments and diplomatic missions worldwide. The business model is elegantly simple — governments outsource the administrative processing of visa, passport, and consular applications to BLS, which operates Visa Application Centres (VACs) on their behalf, collecting fees from applicants.

**Business Segments:**

1. **Visa & Consular Services (~78% of FY25 revenue, declining to ~61% in 9MFY26 as digital grows):** The core franchise. BLS operates VACs for 46+ client governments across 66+ countries with 50,000+ application centres globally. Services include visa processing, passport services, biometric collection, attestation/apostille, and value-added services (courier, document verification, premium lounge). The company acquired Turkey-based iDATA in July 2024 for ~₹720 Cr, adding 37+ centres in 15+ European countries with ~55-60% EBITDA margins.

2. **Digital Services (~22% of FY25 revenue, rising to ~39% in 9MFY26):** Operated through subsidiary BLS E-Services Ltd (listed Feb 2024). This segment provides e-governance services, Business Correspondent (BC) services for banks, Aadhaar enrolment, and citizen services across India through physical touchpoints in rural and semi-urban areas. This segment is growing rapidly (109% YoY in Q3FY26), driven by the Aadifidelis Solutions consolidation.

**Would Buffett Understand It?** Absolutely. This is a classic **toll bridge business** — every person who wants a visa to travel must pass through BLS's processing centres and pay a fee. The company doesn't take travel risk, credit risk, or inventory risk. It collects fees upfront, operates on an asset-light model with leased centres, and benefits from the secular growth in global mobility. Visa charges are collected before services are rendered, creating negative working capital — a hallmark of businesses Buffett loves.

> *"The key to investing is not assessing how much an industry is going to affect society, or how much it will grow, but rather determining the competitive advantage of any given company and, above all, the durability of that advantage."* — Warren Buffett

BLS is a **government-contracted toll collector** on global human mobility. The contracts are typically multi-year (3-5 years) with high renewal rates, creating revenue visibility. The switching costs for governments are enormous — changing visa processors involves security clearances, technology integration, staff training, and diplomatic considerations.

## 10. COMPETITIVE POSITION & MOAT ANALYSIS

### Moat Type: **Government Contract Toll Bridge + Switching Costs + Regulatory Barrier**

**Moat Sources:**

1. **Government Contracts as Toll Bridge:** Every visa applicant for BLS's client governments MUST process through a BLS centre. There is no alternative channel. This is not a consumer choice business — it is a government-mandated processing monopoly for the specific contract geography. BLS currently serves 46+ governments across 66+ countries.

2. **Enormous Switching Costs:** Governments are extremely reluctant to switch visa processors. The reasons are: security clearances and background checks take 12-18 months; technology integration with each government's internal systems is deeply embedded; staff training on each country's specific requirements is substantial; and the reputational risk of a botched transition (imagine a security breach during a visa processor changeover) makes bureaucrats profoundly risk-averse. Contract renewal rates are very high.

3. **Regulatory & Security Barriers:** Handling sovereign citizen data (biometrics, passport information, travel history) requires the highest security clearances. New entrants face a multi-year, multi-million dollar investment just to qualify for their first contract. This is a quasi-regulated monopoly.

4. **Scale Advantage:** BLS's 50,000+ centres and 66-country presence give it a cost and capability advantage when bidding for new contracts. Governments prefer operators with proven global track records.

5. **Network Effects (Weak but Present):** Each new government contract makes BLS more attractive to the next government — credibility compounds.

### Key Competitors

|---|---|---|---|
| VFS Global (Kuoni/EQT) | Global | Manageable — #1 player, but market is growing | Market leader; privately held; BLS is #2 |

The visa outsourcing market is effectively a **duopoly** between VFS Global and BLS International, with TLScontact as a distant third. The market itself is growing at ~14% CAGR (from $3.7B to $8.3B by 2028) and the share of outsourced processing is rising from 35% to an expected 50%. This means both players can grow without taking share from each other — the pie is expanding.

### Is the Business Susceptible to Change?

**No.** This is exactly the kind of business Buffett favours — one where the fundamental model will look the same in 10-20 years. People will still need visas to travel. Governments will still want to outsource the administrative burden. Biometric technology may evolve, but BLS will adopt it (they already offer facial recognition, AI-based processing, etc.). The fundamental economic model — collect fees to process government paperwork — is timeless.

The only disruption risk is if governments bring processing back in-house, but the trend is overwhelmingly in the opposite direction (outsourcing share rising from 22% to 50% over 15 years). Budget constraints and efficiency demands make in-sourcing increasingly unlikely.

> *"We do not get enthused about change... we're looking for the absence of change to protect ways that are already making a lot of money."* — Warren Buffett. BLS's visa processing model is about as change-resistant as it gets.
