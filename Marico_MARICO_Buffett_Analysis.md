## 1. THE BUSINESS — Understanding the Economic Engine

Marico is a classic Buffett-style **consumer franchise** — a business built on daily-use, repeat-purchase consumer staples with dominant brand positions. Founded in 1988 by Harsh Mariwala (who carved it out of the family's Bombay Oil Industries), Marico operates across hair care, edible oils, healthy foods, male grooming, skin care, and fabric care in 25+ countries.

The business model is elegant in its simplicity: buy commodity raw materials (copra, vegetable oils), process and brand them, and sell through a massive distribution network reaching over 5 million retail outlets in India. The branded consumer product sits at the intersection of a **toll bridge** (consumers pay a recurring premium for trusted brands in personal care) and a **consumer franchise** (brand loyalty drives repeat purchases with minimal switching).

**Key Brands & Market Positions (Q3 FY24 data):**
- **Parachute Coconut Oil:** 62% market share, #1 (the iconic blue bottle — India's largest FMCG brand by volume)
- **Saffola Oats:** 41% market share, #1
- **Value Added Hair Oils:** 27% share, #1 (Hair & Care, Parachute Advansed, Nihar)
- **Leave-on Serums (Livon):** 53% share, #1
- **Hair Gels/Waxes (Set Wet):** 52% share, #1

95% of Marico's brands hold the #1 or #2 position in their categories. This is the hallmark of a business Buffett would understand in five minutes.

> *"I try to buy stock in businesses that are so wonderful that an idiot can run them. Because sooner or later, one will."* — Warren Buffett

Marico's coconut oil business is as close to this ideal as any Indian FMCG company. Parachute has been the dominant coconut oil brand for five decades. The product is simple, the moat is deep (brand trust + distribution), and the economics are durable.

**International Presence:** ~22-25% of revenues come from international operations across Bangladesh, Vietnam, South Africa, MENA, and other emerging markets — providing a meaningful growth vector.

## 10. COMPETITIVE POSITION & MOAT ANALYSIS

**Moat Type: Consumer Brand Franchise + Distribution Scale Advantage**

Marico's moat is a powerful combination of brand dominance and distribution depth, reinforced by switching costs in daily-use personal care categories.

**Moat Sources:**

1. **Brand Dominance in Coconut Oil:** Parachute's 62% market share in coconut oils is one of the highest single-brand shares in any Indian FMCG category. This was built over 50+ years and is defended by deep consumer trust — coconut oil goes on hair and skin, categories where consumers are reluctant to experiment with unknown brands. Switching costs are psychological but very real.

2. **Distribution Moat:** Marico reaches 5 million+ retail outlets directly, including deep rural penetration. Replicating this distribution infrastructure would cost thousands of crores and take a decade. Through Project SETU, the company is expanding direct reach further, making the distribution moat wider.

3. **Category #1 Position Across 6 Categories:** Being the market leader in six separate categories provides diversification AND cross-selling leverage. Retailers stock Marico products because consumers demand them — this gives Marico pricing power and shelf space dominance.

## 21. REGULATORY RISKS & POLICY HEADWINDS

Marico operates in a relatively benign regulatory environment. FMCG consumer staples face minimal regulatory intervention compared to tobacco, alcohol, or pharma. Key regulatory considerations:

**GST Impact:** Hair oils were re-classified from 18% GST to 5% GST in some categories — this is a TAILWIND, improving affordability and potentially boosting volumes. Marico has been a beneficiary of GST rationalisation.

**Copra Pricing:** The government sets minimum support prices for copra, which creates a floor under Marico's primary input cost. This is a structural cost pressure but manageable through pricing pass-through.
