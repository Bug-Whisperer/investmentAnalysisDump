## 1. THE BUSINESS — Understanding the Economic Engine

Sonata Software is a mid-cap Indian IT services company, primarily a **Microsoft ecosystem specialist** that delivers digital transformation, cloud modernisation, and application services to global enterprises. The company operates across two distinct segments:

1. **International IT Services (IITS):** The growth engine — offshore/onsite IT services delivering cloud, data, AI, Dynamics 365, Salesforce, and application modernisation to clients primarily in the US, Europe, and Australia. This is where the margin resides.
2. **Domestic Technology Solutions (formerly TMD/DTS):** A high-revenue, low-margin business distributing Microsoft, Oracle, and other technology licences to Indian enterprises. This segment drives top-line but contributes thin margins — essentially a pass-through distribution business.

The business model is relatively simple to understand: Sonata helps enterprises modernise their technology stacks, with deep expertise in the Microsoft ecosystem (Azure, Dynamics 365, Power Platform, Fabric). It leverages its proprietary **Platformation™** framework and the newer **Harmoni.AI** platform for AI-driven modernisation.

Buffett: *"I try to buy stock in businesses that are so wonderful that an idiot can run them. Because sooner or later, one will."* IT services businesses are people-dependent and relationship-driven — not quite the "toll bridge" or "consumer franchise" Buffett prefers. However, Sonata's deep Microsoft partnership and vertical-specific platforms create moderate switching costs. The domestic distribution business is essentially a toll bridge on Microsoft licence sales in India — low-margin but highly predictable.

**Key Corporate Action:** Sonata executed a 1:1 bonus issue in FY2024, doubling shares from ~14 Cr to ~28 Cr. In February 2023, it made its largest-ever acquisition — Quant Systems Inc. (Texas, USA) — expanding capabilities in data analytics, cloud modernisation, and cybersecurity. This acquisition significantly altered the balance sheet and P&L profile.

**Recent Development (April 2026):** CEO transition announced — Samir Dhir stepping down; Rajsekhar Datta Roy appointed as new CEO effective May 9, 2026. Leadership transitions at mid-cap IT companies are a risk factor to monitor.

Buffett: *"In the business world, the rearview mirror is always clearer than the windshield."* The rearview shows strong revenue growth but margin headwinds. ## 10. COMPETITIVE POSITION & MOAT ANALYSIS

**Moat Type:** Moderate — a combination of **switching costs** (deep client integrations on Microsoft stack) and **ecosystem specialisation** (Microsoft partnership advantage).

**Moat Assessment:**

1. **Microsoft Ecosystem Specialisation:** Sonata is among the first companies recognised as a Microsoft Frontier Partner (March 2026). This deep partnership provides early access to new Microsoft technologies, co-selling opportunities, and credibility with enterprises. However, this is a "shared moat" — many IT companies claim Microsoft expertise, and the partnership itself can be replicated.

2. **Switching Costs:** Once Sonata has built and deployed a Dynamics 365 or Azure solution for a client, switching to another vendor involves significant time, cost, and risk.
