## 1. THE BUSINESS — Understanding the Economic Engine

Info Edge is India's premier online classifieds company, built around four verticals: **Naukri.com** (online recruitment, ~74% of revenue), **99acres.com** (real estate classifieds, ~16%), **Jeevansathi.com** (matrimonial), and **Shiksha.com** (education). Beyond operations, it holds a massive investment portfolio (~₹48,000 Cr fair value) anchored by stakes in **Zomato** (~12.4%) and **PB Fintech/Policybazaar** (~19%).

**Would Buffett understand it?** Absolutely. The core Naukri business is a **toll bridge**. Every company hiring white-collar talent in India pays Naukri a toll to access its 82 million+ resume database. Recruiters cannot afford NOT to be on Naukri — it has ~80% traffic share in the Indian e-recruitment space. This is a textbook **network-effects-plus-switching-costs** franchise: the more resumes on the platform, the more recruiters come; the more recruiters post, the more job-seekers register. The database becomes more valuable every year, compounding the moat.

99acres, Jeevansathi, and Shiksha are weaker franchises — competitive, lower-margin, and still maturing. The investment arm (Zomato, PB Fintech, 100+ startups) is a separate value pool entirely — think of it as Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway investment portfolio sitting alongside operating businesses.

> **Buffett:** *"The key to investing is not assessing how much an industry is going to affect society, or how much it will grow, but rather determining the competitive advantage of any given company and, above all, the durability of that advantage."*

Naukri's competitive advantage — its massive resume database and recruiter network — has been durable for over two decades. This is a franchise Buffett would recognise immediately.

## 7. COMPETITIVE POSITION & MOAT ANALYSIS

### Moat Assessment

**Naukri.com — Wide Moat, Enduring**

- **Network Effects (Primary Moat):** 82 million+ resumes, 72,100+ unique paying clients, ~80% traffic share. The more resumes on the platform, the more essential it becomes for employers. This is a self-reinforcing flywheel that has strengthened over 25+ years.

- **Switching Costs:** Recruiters build their entire hiring workflow around Naukri — Resdex (resume database), applicant tracking, employer branding. Switching to a competitor means losing access to the largest talent pool and rebuilding processes.

- **Brand:** "Naukri" is essentially a synonym for "job search" in India, similar to how "Xerox" became synonymous with photocopying. This is priceless.

- **Data Moat:** 25+ years of resume data, hiring patterns, and salary benchmarks. This data powers AI-driven matching, pricing algorithms, and new products — and it only gets richer with time.

**99acres — Narrow Moat, Improving**

Real estate classifieds is more competitive (MagicBricks, Housing.com, NoBroker). 99acres has good traffic share but not Naukri-level dominance. The business is approaching profitability, which is a positive signal.

**Jeevansathi — No Moat**

Distant third behind Shaadi.com and BharatMatrimony. The matrimonial space is highly competitive and commoditised. This is not a franchise business.

### Key Competitors & Threats

| Competitor | Segment | Threat Level | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | Recruitment | Manageable (currently) | Strong globally but limited India traction for volume hiring. Bigger threat in premium/executive segment. |
| Indeed / Naukri Gulf competitors | Recruitment | Low | Naukri's India moat is too deep. |
| MagicBricks / NoBroker | Real Estate | Moderate | Real estate classifieds remain competitive; NoBroker's disintermediation model is different. |
| AI-native hiring platforms | Recruitment | Medium-term risk | AI could change HOW hiring happens. Naukri is investing heavily in AI (Talent Pulse, AI matching). |
| Google for Jobs | Recruitment | Low-to-Moderate | Aggregation threat exists but hasn't materialised meaningfully in India. |

### Susceptibility to Change

This is where Buffett would be thoughtful. The core question: **will companies still need a centralised platform to find talent 10-20 years from now?**

The answer is almost certainly yes — the fundamental need for employers to find candidates and vice versa is enduring. The HOW may evolve (AI matching, automated screening, etc.), but the platform that has the largest database of candidates has an enormous structural advantage in any technological paradigm.

However, **the risk of AI disruption is real.** If large language models enable candidate-employer matching without needing a centralised database (e.g., candidates' professional data is everywhere on LinkedIn, GitHub, etc.), Naukri's database moat could erode. Management is clearly aware of this and investing in AI capabilities.

> **Buffett:** *"We do not get enthused about change, with a few exceptions, as a way to make a lot of money. We're looking for the absence of change to protect ways that are already making a lot of money."*

The recruitment classifieds model has been remarkably stable for 25+ years. The shift from newspapers to online already happened — and Naukri won that war decisively. The next shift (AI-native hiring) is the one to watch, but Naukri's data advantage gives it a strong starting position.

## 9. MANAGEMENT QUALITY & BUFFETT'S $1 TEST

### Buffett's $1 Test

This test is complicated for Info Edge because retained earnings went overwhelmingly into the investment portfolio (Zomato, PB Fintech, startups), and the market cap is heavily influenced by the value of those investments.

From FY2018 to FY2025, the company retained approximately ₹14,000+ Cr (including massive unrealised gains). Market cap went from ~₹22,000 Cr to ~₹63,500 Cr — an increase of ~₹41,500 Cr. However, much of the retained earnings are paper gains on investments.

If we focus on the INVESTMENT TRACK RECORD: Info Edge invested ₹3,959 Cr over 15 years and the portfolio is worth ₹36,855 Cr — a 9.3x return, 36% gross IRR (per management's FY25 annual letter). **This is world-class capital allocation.** Every ₹1 invested in startups has become ₹9.3.

> *Note: The 36% gross IRR is higher than a simple CAGR on the total amount (9.3x over 15 years = ~16% CAGR) because IRR accounts for the timing of investments — early bets like Zomato (₹86 Cr in 2010-2013) had much longer to compound than recent investments. The IRR is management's own calculation from their shareholder letter.*

### Capital Allocation Track Record

- **Startup investing:** Outstanding. Zomato (₹86 Cr invested → worth ₹20,000+ Cr) and PB Fintech are generational bets. The 36% IRR across the portfolio is exceptional.
- **Operating business reinvestment:** Smart. Naukri's product suite has expanded (Talent Pulse, Enterprise Resdex, AmbitionBox, JobHai) without wasteful spending.
- **Subsidiary businesses:** 99acres is finally approaching profitability after years of investment. Jeevansathi has been a value destroyer but losses are now contained. Coding Ninjas acquisition is still being digested.
- **Dividends:** Increasing payout — the board raised the dividend policy to up to 65% of standalone profits. Sensible given the operating business generates more cash than it can deploy.
- **Stock split (2025):** 1:5 split to improve liquidity and retail access. Neutral from a value perspective.
- **No buybacks:** The company has never done buybacks. Given the stock has often traded at high multiples, this is arguably disciplined — not buying back expensive stock is smart.

### Management Quality

Founder-led by **Sanjeev Bikhchandani** (Executive Vice-Chairman, 37.6% promoter stake). This is meaningful skin in the game. Bikhchandani is widely respected as one of India's most visionary and disciplined internet entrepreneurs. **Hitesh Oberoi** serves as MD & CEO.

- CFO transition: Chintan Thakkar (long-serving CFO) departed in late 2025, replaced by Ambarish Raghuvanshi as interim CFO. This is a minor yellow flag worth monitoring, but the transition appears smooth.
- Employee count: ~6,150 — lean for the scale of business.
- SBC: Not a major concern — Info Edge is not a Silicon Valley-style SBC-heavy company. Employee costs are predominantly cash-based.
- Communication: Annual reports and con-call transcripts are detailed and candid. Bikhchandani's shareholder letters are thoughtful and honest.

> **Buffett:** *"I try to buy stock in businesses that are so wonderful that an idiot can run them. Because sooner or later, one will."*

Naukri is close to this ideal — the moat is so wide that the business practically runs itself. But credit to management for the extraordinary investment portfolio, which required genuine skill and courage.
