## 1. THE BUSINESS — Understanding the Economic Engine

Kalyan Jewellers India Ltd is one of India's largest organised jewellery retailers, founded in 1993 by T.S. Kalyanaraman in Thrissur, Kerala. The company designs, manufactures, and sells gold, diamond, and precious stone jewellery across various price points through a hyperlocal retail model.

**Business Model Simplicity:** Buffett would understand this business in five minutes. Buy gold (the raw material), manufacture jewellery (add making charges), sell to consumers at a markup. Revenue is primarily driven by gold price passthrough plus a making charge margin of 8-15% on studded and 6-10% on gold jewellery. It is a **consumer retail franchise** — a brand-driven, trust-based business in a sector where trust is everything.

**What Kind of Business Is This?**

This is a **consumer franchise with switching costs.** Jewellery is an emotional, high-value, trust-driven purchase. Customers don't switch jewellers lightly — they buy where they trust the purity, the craftsmanship, and the exchange/buyback policies. Kalyan has built this trust over three decades through hallmarking (before it was mandated), transparent pricing (rate tags since 2007), and regional brand ambassadors.

> *Buffett: "The key to investing is determining the competitive advantage of any given company and, above all, the durability of that advantage. The products or services that have wide, sustainable moats around them are the ones that deliver rewards to investors."*

The economic moat here is **brand trust + hyperlocal presence + the organised-vs-unorganised shift.** As India moves from unorganised (karigars, family jewellers) to organised retail — driven by hallmarking mandates, GST formalisation, and consumer preference for transparency — Kalyan is a direct beneficiary.

**Operating Model (as of Q3 FY26):**
- **Total Showrooms:** ~436+ globally (287 India, 36 Middle East, 2 USA, 110+ Candere outlets)
- **Franchise-Owned Company-Operated (FOCO) Model:** 174 FOCO showrooms in India, 4 in Middle East — this is the asset-light engine driving growth
- **Candere:** Online-first lifestyle jewellery brand, turned PAT-positive in Q3 FY26 with 144% revenue growth
- **Non-South India:** Now contributes ~58.5% of India revenue — the growth runway is massive

### Trend Narrative

The story is one of **dramatic transformation.** Pre-FY22, Kalyan was a mediocre, leveraged, low-margin jewellery retailer with losses in FY19 and FY21. Post-IPO (March 2021), the business has inflected sharply:

1. **Revenue has nearly tripled** from ₹8,573 Cr (FY21) to ₹25,045 Cr (FY25), and TTM is tracking ~₹31,650 Cr.
2. **Profit has gone from losses to ₹714 Cr** in FY25 and TTM ~₹1,129 Cr (including the blockbuster Q3 FY26).
3. **Operating margins are thin but stable** at 6-8% — this is characteristic of jewellery retail where gold passthrough inflates revenue. The real metric is absolute EBITDA growth, which has been excellent.
4. **Q3 FY26 was spectacular:** Revenue ₹10,343 Cr (+42% YoY), EBITDA ₹750.5 Cr (+75% YoY), PAT ₹416 Cr (+90% YoY), EBITDA margin improved to 7.3% from 5.9%.
5. **Candere turned PAT-positive** with 144% revenue growth — a crucial development for the digital/lifestyle pivot.

**Critical Point:** The thin 6-8% operating margin is NOT a weakness for this business. In jewellery retail, the vast majority of revenue is gold passthrough. A ~6% margin on ₹25,000 Cr revenue is ₹1,585 Cr of operating profit — what matters is the absolute profit and its growth rate. Gross margins are structurally limited by gold content, and Kalyan's improving studded jewellery mix (31.2% in Q3 FY26 vs 29.5% prior year) is the key lever for margin expansion.

> *Buffett: "I look for businesses that I think I can predict what they'll look like in 10 or 15 or 20 years. That means businesses that will look more or less as they do today — except that they'll be larger and doing more business internationally."*

Kalyan's business in 2036 will look exactly like today — selling gold and diamond jewellery to Indian consumers — except it will have 800+ stores instead of 400+, and revenue will likely be ₹60,000-80,000 Cr. This predictability is valuable.

### Buffett's Financial Fortress Assessment

**Strengths:**
- **Debt/Equity has halved** from 2.27x (FY18) to 1.03x (FY25) — dramatic deleveraging while growing revenue 2.4x.
- **Inventory days improved dramatically** from 272 (FY21 worst) to 162 (FY25 best) — the company is becoming far more capital-efficient.
- **Cash conversion cycle improved** from 241 days to 129 days — nearly halved.
- **Management targets debt-free status by FY27** — reduced debt by ₹400 Cr in FY25, targeting ₹300 Cr more in FY26.
- **Book value per share** growing at 14-16% CAGR — compounding equity steadily.

**Concerns:**
- **Borrowings at ₹4,959 Cr are still substantial** — much of this is Gold Metal Loans (GML), which are a characteristic feature of jewellery businesses and are collateralised by gold inventory. Not comparable to typical corporate debt.

- **Goodwill/Intangibles:** Not material — tangible book value ≈ book value. This is a real-asset business.

> *Buffett: "We will reject interesting opportunities rather than over-leverage our balance sheet."*

The balance sheet is **improving but not yet a fortress.** The deleveraging trajectory is positive, and if management achieves debt-free status by FY27, this concern evaporates. The FOCO model is the key enabler — franchise-led expansion requires far less capital than owned stores.

## 7. COMPETITIVE POSITION & MOAT ANALYSIS

### The Indian Jewellery Battlefield

The Indian jewellery market is estimated at ~USD 85-95 billion (₹7-8 lakh crore), with the organised sector at ~36-38% and growing. Kalyan commands ~6% of the organised market and ~2% of the overall market.

### Key Competitors

| Competitor | Market Cap | Revenue (FY25E) | Key Strength | Threat Level |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Titan (Tanishq)** | ~₹3.7 lakh Cr | ~₹50,000+ Cr | Premium brand, Tata backing, design | Manageable (different positioning) |
| **Malabar Gold** | Unlisted | ~₹50,000+ Cr | Aggressive expansion, Middle East | Manageable |
| **Joyalukkas** | Unlisted | ~₹20,000+ Cr | South India + Middle East | Moderate |
| **Senco Gold** | ~₹6,000 Cr | ~₹6,000+ Cr | East India strong | Low |
| **Unorganised** | — | ~₹4-5 lakh Cr | Local trust, no GST | The real competitor, but shrinking |

### Moat Assessment

**Type of Moat:** Brand Trust + Scale Economics Shared with Customers + Regulatory Tailwind

1. **Brand Trust (Strong, Widening):** Kalyan was the first major jeweller to offer BIS hallmarking voluntarily (2003), transparent rate tags (2007), and a nationwide exchange/buyback policy. In a market where consumers have been cheated by local jewellers for generations, this trust is hard-earned and hard to replicate.

2. **Hyperlocal Presence (Strong):** 436+ showrooms with a FOCO model that allows rapid penetration into Tier 2-4 cities where unorganised jewellers dominate. The "My Kalyan" network of customer outreach centres embeds the brand in local communities.

3. **Regulatory Tailwind (Strong, Structural):** Mandatory hallmarking, GST formalisation, and customs duty rationalisation are systematically destroying the unorganised sector's advantages (underkarating, tax evasion). This is a one-way tide favouring organised players.

4. **Switching Costs (Moderate):** Gold exchange and advance purchase schemes create stickiness. Customers who have ongoing schemes with Kalyan are unlikely to switch.

5. **Network Effects (Emerging):** The Candere digital platform and Kalyan's own app create data-driven customer relationships that compound over time.

### Is the Moat Enduring or Eroding?

**Enduring and widening.** The organised-to-unorganised shift is a multi-decade structural trend. Mandatory hallmarking, digital payments (UPI at jewellers), and GST compliance are permanent changes that favour Kalyan. The FOCO model accelerates expansion without diluting the moat.

### Susceptibility to Change

> *Buffett: "We're looking for the absence of change to protect ways that are already making a lot of money and even more in the future."*

Jewellery retail has been essentially unchanged for centuries. People buy gold and diamonds for weddings, festivals, and investment. The delivery channel has evolved (from local karigars to showrooms to omnichannel), but the fundamental consumer behaviour is deeply embedded in Indian culture. Gold jewellery demand in India is driven by marriages (~50% of consumption), festivals, and store of value — none of these drivers are going away in the next 20 years.

**Disruption risk is LOW.** Lab-grown diamonds may disrupt the diamond segment marginally, but gold jewellery (Kalyan's core) is not substitutable. Digital-first jewellers (CaratLane/Titan, BlueStone) are a factor in the lightweight/fashion segment but not in the core traditional jewellery market.

**Verdict: The moat is moderate-to-strong and widening.** Not a Coca-Cola-level moat (no network effects, limited pricing power given gold passthrough), but a solid and improving competitive position in a massive, growing market.

## 9. MANAGEMENT QUALITY & BUFFETT'S $1 TEST

### The Kalyanaraman Family

The company is founder-led by T.S. Kalyanaraman (Chairman & MD), with sons T.K. Seetharam and T.K. Ramesh (Whole-Time Directors) in executive roles. Promoter holding is a strong **62.75%** (Dec 2025).

### Buffett's $1 Test

**For every ₹1 of retained earnings, has the company created at least ₹1 of market value?**

Over the FY23-FY25 period, cumulative net profit was ~₹1,742 Cr, with estimated dividends of ~₹334 Cr, leaving ~₹1,408 Cr of retained earnings (~₹13.7 per share). The stock price rose from ~₹85 (start of FY23) to ₹380 currently, creating ~₹295 of market value per share. This implies **~₹21.6 of market value per ₹1 of retained earnings** — the $1 test is passed with flying colours.

**Caveat:** Much of this reflects PE multiple expansion from ~20x to ~35x. The underlying business performance supports this, but investors should be aware that some of the value creation is from re-rating, not just compounding.

### Capital Allocation Track Record

- **Debt reduction:** Reduced debt by ₹400 Cr in FY25, targeting ₹300 Cr more in FY26, with net-debt-free target by FY27. **Excellent discipline.**
- **FOCO pivot:** Shifting from capital-heavy owned stores to asset-light franchise model. **Smart capital allocation.**
