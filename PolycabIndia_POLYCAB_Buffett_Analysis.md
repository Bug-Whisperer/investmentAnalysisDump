## 1. THE BUSINESS — Understanding the Economic Engine

Polycab India is India's **largest manufacturer of wires and cables**, holding a dominant 26–27% share of the organised domestic W&C market. Founded in 1964 and listed in April 2019, the company operates through three segments:

1. **Wires & Cables (W&C) — ~84% of FY25 revenue:** The core cash engine. Polycab manufactures 10,600+ SKUs spanning flexible wires, building wires, power cables, control cables, optical fibre cables, rubber cables, and specialty cables. The business serves both retail/distribution channels (household wiring, construction) and institutional/B2B channels (infrastructure, industrial, utilities). This is a **toll-bridge business** — every building, factory, solar farm, data centre, and EV charging station needs wires and cables. Polycab collects the toll.

2. **Fast Moving Electrical Goods (FMEG) — ~9% of FY25 revenue:** A high-growth flanker strategy. Polycab leverages its brand and distribution network (4,700+ authorised dealers, 2,00,000+ retail touchpoints) to sell fans, LED lighting, switches, switchgear, water heaters, solar products, and pumps. This is a **consumer franchise** play — using the trust built in W&C to sell higher-margin consumer electricals.

3. **EPC (Engineering, Procurement & Construction) — ~7% of FY25 revenue:** Turnkey power distribution, transmission, and rural electrification projects. This is a **project business** — lumpy, lower-margin, but it deepens relationships with state utilities and provides visibility into infrastructure spending trends.

Buffett: *"I try to buy stock in businesses that are so wonderful that an idiot can run them. Because sooner or later, one will."* Polycab's W&C business is about as simple as it gets — buy copper and aluminium, draw it into wire, insulate it, and sell it through a massive distribution network into non-discretionary demand. Every house needs wiring. Every factory needs cables. The product hasn't fundamentally changed in decades, and demand is tied to electrification, construction, and infrastructure — all structural growth drivers in India. This is squarely within Buffett's circle of competence.

## 10. COMPETITIVE POSITION & MOAT ANALYSIS

**Moat Type: Cost Advantage + Brand + Distribution Network (Wide Moat)**

**The Castle:** India's #1 wires & cables company with ~27% organised market share, 10,600+ SKUs, 27+ manufacturing plants, 4,700+ authorised dealers, and 200,000+ retail touchpoints.

**The Moat — Multi-layered:**

**1. Scale-Driven Cost Advantage:** As the largest manufacturer, Polycab enjoys: (a) superior procurement terms for copper and aluminium (the primary raw materials, comprising 60–70% of costs); (b) manufacturing efficiency from higher utilisation across a diversified plant network; (c) logistics efficiency from strategically located plants across Gujarat, Maharashtra, Uttarakhand, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Daman. Smaller competitors cannot match these unit economics.

**2. Brand & Trust Franchise:** In wires and cables — where product failure means fire risk and safety hazard — brand trust is paramount. Polycab's brand carries a safety premium that allows pricing power. Electricians and contractors (the key purchase influencers in the retail channel) default to Polycab because their reputation is on the line.

**3. Distribution Depth:** 4,700+ dealers and 200,000+ retail points create a distribution web that is extraordinarily expensive and time-consuming to replicate.
