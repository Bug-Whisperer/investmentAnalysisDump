## The Filter: What Makes an Industry "Ace Investor Grade"?

Both Buffett and Jhunjhunwala (and investors like Dolly Khanna, Vijay Kedia, Ashish Dhawan, Radhakishan Damani) converge on the same core principles when picking industries, even if their styles differ in aggression:

1. **High ROCE (>20%)** — The business must earn returns above its cost of capital, consistently. This is the single most important number.
2. **High OPM (>15%)** — Decent operating margins suggest pricing power and a moat. Thin margins mean commodity exposure.
3. **Reasonable PE** — Not overpriced relative to growth. They don't chase expensive fads.
4. **Structural Growth** — Secular demand tailwinds (India's demographics, urbanisation, consumption upgrade, formalisation).
5. **Absence of Change** — Buffett specifically avoids industries facing rapid disruption. He wants the business model to look the same in 10-20 years.
6. **Compounding Ability** — Can the companies in this industry reinvest profits at high rates for decades?

## THE INDUSTRIES ACE INVESTORS WOULD AVOID

Equally important — industries Buffett and Jhunjhunwala would stay AWAY from:

| Industry | Why They'd Avoid It |
|---|---|
| Airlines (17x PE, 17% ROCE) | Buffett famously said airlines have destroyed more capital than they've created. Capital-intensive, no pricing power, one crisis away from bankruptcy. |
| E-Retail / E-Commerce (38x PE, -3% ROCE) | Negative returns on capital. Cash-burning. No proven path to durable profitability for most players. Buffett: "If you've been playing poker for half an hour and you still don't know who the patsy is, you're the patsy." |
| Civil Construction (15x PE, 16% ROCE) | Appears cheap but working capital hell, lumpy earnings, government payment delays. Jhunjhunwala avoided pure construction companies. |
| Telecom (35x PE, 13% ROCE) | Massive capex, regulatory risk, price wars. Only 2-3 survivors in India. Winner-take-all but the "winning" is mostly going to debt servicing. |
| Real Estate (26x PE, 10% ROCE) | Capital-guzzler, opaque accounting, governance concerns. Jhunjhunwala had very selective real estate exposure. |
| Hotels & Resorts (28x PE, 15% ROCE) | Cyclical, capital-intensive, perishable inventory (empty room = lost revenue forever). Buffett avoids hotels. |
