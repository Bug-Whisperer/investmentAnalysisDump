## 1. THE BUSINESS — Understanding the Economic Engine

Cummins India Ltd is the Indian subsidiary of Cummins Inc., USA (51% promoter holding). Established in 1962 and headquartered in Pune, the company designs, manufactures, distributes, and services diesel and alternative-fuel engines (2.8 to 100 litres), power generator sets (up to 3,000 kW / 3,750 kVA), and related components and technology. It operates through three business units: **Engine**, **Power Systems**, and **Distribution**.

**Business Model in Buffett Terms:** This is a **technology-backed industrial franchise** with significant **switching costs** and **aftermarket annuity revenue**. Once an OEM designs a vehicle or genset around a Cummins engine, switching to a competitor is expensive and time-consuming (re-engineering, re-certification, new supply chains). The aftermarket spare parts and service business provides a recurring revenue stream with high margins — a classic Buffett "toll bridge" on installed base.

The company serves critical end-markets: infrastructure, construction, mining, data centres, commercial buildings, defence, marine, power generation (including standby/prime power), and industrial applications. These are essential sectors that will grow with India's industrialisation and urbanisation — a secular tailwind.

Buffett: *"I try to buy stock in businesses that are so wonderful that an idiot can run them. Because sooner or later, one will."* Cummins India's moat doesn't depend on managerial genius — it's embedded in the technology, brand, installed base, and distribution network. The parent's 51% ownership ensures technology access and governance alignment.

Would Buffett understand this business? **Absolutely.** Engines and power generation are tangible, necessary, and understandable. Cummins makes things that make other things run. The business model is clear: sell engines and gensets, then profit from decades of aftermarket service on the installed base. Circle of competence: ✅

## 10. COMPETITIVE POSITION & MOAT ANALYSIS

**Moat Type:** Multi-layered — **Technology + Brand + Switching Costs + Distribution Network + Aftermarket Lock-In**

**Moat Sources:**

**1. Technology & IP:** Cummins Inc. (parent) invests ~$1B+ annually in R&D globally. Cummins India gets access to this technology at arm's-length royalty rates (~2-3% of revenue). No Indian competitor can match this R&D investment. The company is at the forefront of emission compliance (BS-VI/CPCB-IV+), alternative fuels (natural gas, hydrogen), and digital/connected engines.

**2. Brand & Reputation:** The Cummins brand is synonymous with reliability in power generation and heavy-duty engines across India. Decades of customer trust, particularly in mission-critical applications (hospitals, data centres, telecom towers, defence), create a premium brand moat.

**3. Switching Costs:** Once an OEM designs a vehicle, genset, or equipment around a Cummins engine, the switching cost is substantial — re-engineering, re-certification, re-training service staff. The installed base of engines creates decades of locked-in aftermarket demand.

**4. Distribution & Service Network:** Over 500+ dealer and service touchpoints across India. This distribution reach is extremely expensive and time-consuming to replicate.

**5. Aftermarket Annuity:** The installed base of engines and gensets generates recurring revenue from spare parts, maintenance, and rebuilds. This is high-margin, predictable revenue that grows as the installed base grows — a powerful compounding mechanism.
