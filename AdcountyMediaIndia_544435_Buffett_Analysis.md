## 1. THE BUSINESS — Understanding the Economic Engine

Adcounty Media India Ltd (AMIL) is a **BrandTech / AdTech company** incorporated in 2017, headquartered in Gurugram (Jaipur-registered). The business model is straightforward: it helps brands find, target, acquire, and retain customers through digital advertising technology. The company operates across three revenue streams:

1. **Programmatic Advertising Platform (BidCounty):** A proprietary platform supporting automated, data-driven ad buying for both performance (CPA, CPS, CPL) and branding campaigns.
2. **Digital Marketing Solutions:** End-to-end services including SEO, PPC, connected TV (CTV) ads, and performance marketing for clients across FinTech, BFSI, E-Commerce, Gaming, Travel, and FMCG sectors.
3. **Content Websites & Apps:** Portfolio of owned digital properties — OfferCounty.com (coupons), WealthCounty.com (financial content), RealMoney-Games.com (online gaming) — that generate traffic which is monetised via advertising.

Additional platforms under development include **VOOHOO Live, Genwin, iSearch Ads, SeeTV** (targeted CTV ads for Apple households, launch Q3 2026), **Opsis Ads** (launched Dec 2025), and **PUB 361** (ad-ops/reporting platform integrated with Google Ad Manager 360, launched Feb 2026).

**Would Buffett understand it?** Partially. The digital advertising intermediary model is conceptually simple — the company is a **toll bridge between advertisers and audiences**. Brands pay AMIL to place ads in front of the right consumers; AMIL earns a spread on media spend. However, the AdTech space is technically complex, rapidly evolving, and intensely competitive — characteristics Buffett typically avoids.

> *Buffett: "I try to invest in businesses that are so wonderful that an idiot can run them. Because sooner or later, one will."* This business requires technical skill and constant adaptation — it is NOT an idiot-proof franchise.

**Business Classification:** This is a **services/technology business** with characteristics of both a toll-bridge (intermediating ad spend) and a platform play (proprietary AdTech tools). It is **asset-light** with minimal fixed assets, no inventory, and high operating leverage once scale is achieved.

**Key Clients:** Zepto, ShareChat, Fi Money, PolicyBazaar, and others across 30+ countries (India, Southeast Asia, Middle East, Europe, Latin America).

**Recent Milestones:** IPO in July 2025 (oversubscribed 273x), listed on BSE SME at issue price ₹85/share. Acquired 99.95% stake in Adaxx Adtech & Media LLP (March 2026) for ₹10 Cr. Received "Great Place to Work" India certification (Oct 2025).

OPM has expanded from ~8% in FY2020–21 to ~26% in FY2025, and net margins have nearly quadrupled from ~5–6% to ~20%. **Buffett's "Financial Fortress" Assessment:**

The balance sheet is a **fortress**. The company is virtually debt-free (Debt/Equity = 0.03x) with ₹35.61 Cr of net cash as of Sep 2025 (₹36.61 Cr gross cash minus ~₹1 Cr debt), primarily from IPO proceeds. There is zero long-term debt. The current ratio is exceptionally strong.

**However, two yellow flags require attention:**

1. **Debtor Days at 161 (FY2025):** This is the single biggest balance sheet concern. Receivables have ballooned from ₹3 Cr (FY2021) to ₹30 Cr (FY2025), and debtor days have more than doubled (from 69 to 161). In the digital advertising industry, delayed payments from clients or intermediaries can mask revenue quality issues.

2. **Working Capital Days surging from -37 to +132:** The business has shifted from a negative working capital model to a positive working capital model that is consuming cash. This directly explains why FY2025 operating cash flow was near zero despite ₹13.75 Cr of net profit.

> *Buffett: "In the business world, the rearview mirror is always clearer than the windshield."* The rapid deterioration in debtor days is a forward-looking risk that the profit numbers alone don't reveal.

No goodwill on the balance sheet. Tangible book value equals book value (₹42.26 per share as of Sep 2025).

## 20. TEST SIMPLICITY / NO BRAINER NATURE OF THE INVESTMENT

**Can the thesis be explained to a 10-year-old?**

*"This company helps businesses show their ads to the right people on phones and computers. They make ₹14 for every ₹69 earned. They have no debt and ₹36 Cr sitting in the bank. The stock costs ₹102, but the company earns about ₹8 per share every year — and that number is growing 30-60% annually."*

**Napkin Math:**
- EPS = ₹7.84. At 15% growth for 5 years → ₹15.77
- At 18x PE → ₹284 (+178%, ~23% CAGR)
- At 12x PE → ₹189 (+85%, ~13% CAGR)

> *Buffett: "If you need to use Excel, it's an automatic pass."* The napkin math works convincingly. At 13x earnings for a 30% grower, you don't need a spreadsheet. The only question is whether growth sustains — and that's a judgment call, not a calculation problem.

**Is it a no-brainer?** Almost. The debtor days concern and absence of enduring moat prevent it from being a "close your eyes and buy" situation. It's a **high-conviction buy for the price-aware investor who monitors execution** — not a buy-and-forget Buffett holding.
