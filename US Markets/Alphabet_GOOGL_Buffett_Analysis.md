## 1. THE BUSINESS — Understanding the Economic Engine

Alphabet is the holding company of Google, the world's dominant search engine, and a portfolio of technology businesses spanning digital advertising, cloud computing, autonomous vehicles (Waymo), and experimental ventures. The business is elegantly simple at its core: **Google is the world's largest toll bridge on the information highway.**

Every day, billions of people pass through Google's search engine to find answers. Advertisers pay a toll (cost-per-click) to place their messages in front of these users. This is one of the purest advertising toll bridges in history — users come voluntarily, the "traffic" is intent-based (people searching for things they want to buy), and the bridge gets wider and stickier with every passing year as data compounds.

**Revenue Composition (FY2025):**
- **Google Search & Other:** ~57% of revenue ($228.3B) — the core toll bridge
- **YouTube Ads:** ~10% (~$39.9B) — the world's dominant video platform
- **Google Network:** ~6% (~$25.3B) — ads served on third-party sites
- **Google Subscriptions, Platforms & Devices:** ~11% (~$42.8B) — YouTube Premium, Google One (325M+ paid subs), Pixel
- **Google Cloud:** ~16% (~$65.1B) — enterprise cloud & AI infrastructure, $70B+ annual run rate
- **Other Bets (Waymo etc.):** <1% — optionality

Buffett: *"The key to investing is determining the competitive advantage of any given company and, above all, the durability of that advantage."* Google's advantage — a self-reinforcing flywheel of users, data, and advertisers — has proved extraordinarily durable for over two decades.

Would Buffett understand this business? **Yes.** It is a toll bridge with monopoly-like economics, a consumer franchise with switching costs embedded in habit, and a platform business with network effects. It sits squarely in any thoughtful investor's circle of competence.
